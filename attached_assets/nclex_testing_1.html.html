Nursing RN Study Quiz

1. A patient with a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) presents with increased shortness of breath and wheezing. Which intervention should be prioritized?

  • Administer a sedative
  • Administer a bronchodilator
  • Encourage deep breathing exercises
  • Increase fluid intake
Correct Answer: B

Administering a bronchodilator is crucial for relieving bronchospasm and improving airflow in COPD exacerbations.

2. A patient with diabetes mellitus type 1 is experiencing hypoglycemia. What is the most appropriate initial intervention?

  • Administer insulin
  • Provide a high-protein snack
  • Administer 15g of fast-acting carbohydrate
  • Encourage physical activity
Correct Answer: C

Administering 15g of fast-acting carbohydrate is the recommended initial treatment for hypoglycemia to quickly raise blood glucose levels.

3. A patient with a history of myocardial infarction is prescribed a beta-blocker. What is the primary purpose of this medication?

  • Increase heart rate
  • Increase blood pressure
  • Increase myocardial oxygen demand
  • Decrease myocardial oxygen demand
Correct Answer: D

Beta-blockers decrease myocardial oxygen demand by reducing heart rate and contractility, which is beneficial post-myocardial infarction.

4. A patient with a central line presents with fever and chills. What is the most appropriate initial action?

  • Administer antipyretics
  • Draw blood cultures from the line
  • Remove the central line
  • Start broad-spectrum antibiotics
Correct Answer: B

Draw blood cultures from the line before starting antibiotics to identify the causative organism and guide treatment.

5. A patient with a head injury is being monitored for increased intracranial pressure (ICP). Which assessment finding is most concerning?

  • Decreased level of consciousness
  • Pupil constriction
  • Increased heart rate
  • Decreased blood pressure
Correct Answer: A

A decreased level of consciousness is a critical sign of increased ICP and requires immediate intervention.

6. A patient with a history of asthma is prescribed a corticosteroid inhaler. What is the primary benefit of this medication?

  • Immediate relief of bronchospasm
  • Reduction of airway inflammation
  • Increase in mucus production
  • Increase in airway resistance
Correct Answer: B

Corticosteroids reduce airway inflammation, which helps prevent asthma exacerbations.

7. A patient with a urinary tract infection (UTI) is prescribed an antibiotic. What is the most important nursing action?

  • Monitor for allergic reactions
  • Ensure the patient completes the full course
  • Encourage increased fluid intake
  • Both B and C
Correct Answer: D

Ensuring the patient completes the full course of antibiotics and encouraging increased fluid intake are both crucial for effective treatment of a UTI.

8. A patient with heart failure is prescribed a loop diuretic. What is the primary therapeutic effect?

  • Increase blood pressure
  • Reduce fluid overload
  • Increase potassium levels
  • Increase sodium retention
Correct Answer: B

Loop diuretics reduce fluid overload by promoting diuresis, which is essential in managing heart failure.

9. A patient with a history of peptic ulcer disease is prescribed a proton pump inhibitor (PPI). What is the primary action of this medication?

  • Reduce gastric acid secretion
  • Increase gastric acid secretion
  • Neutralize stomach acid
  • Increase gastric motility
Correct Answer: A

PPIs reduce gastric acid secretion, promoting ulcer healing and preventing recurrence.

10. A patient with a history of stroke is prescribed anticoagulation therapy. What is the primary purpose of this medication?

  • Prevent blood clots
  • Increase blood clotting
  • Reduce blood pressure
  • Increase heart rate
Correct Answer: A

Anticoagulation therapy is used to prevent the formation of blood clots, reducing the risk of recurrent stroke.

11. A patient with chronic kidney disease (CKD) is prescribed a phosphate binder. What is the primary purpose of this medication?

  • Increase phosphate absorption
  • Decrease phosphate absorption
  • Increase calcium absorption
  • Decrease calcium absorption
Correct Answer: B

Phosphate binders decrease phosphate absorption in the gut, helping to manage hyperphosphatemia in CKD patients.

12. A patient with a history of hypertension is prescribed an ACE inhibitor. What is the primary mechanism of action?

  • Increase angiotensin II levels
  • Increase aldosterone secretion
  • Increase blood volume
  • Inhibit the conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II
Correct Answer: D

ACE inhibitors inhibit the conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II, leading to vasodilation and reduced blood pressure.

13. A patient with a history of deep vein thrombosis (DVT) is prescribed compression stockings. What is the primary benefit?

  • Prevent blood clots
  • Increase blood flow to the legs
  • Reduce leg swelling
  • Both A and C
Correct Answer: D

Compression stockings help prevent blood clots and reduce leg swelling by improving venous return.

14. A patient with a history of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) is prescribed an H2 receptor antagonist. What is the primary action of this medication?

  • Increase gastric acid secretion
  • Neutralize stomach acid
  • Reduce gastric acid secretion
  • Increase gastric motility
Correct Answer: C

H2 receptor antagonists reduce gastric acid secretion, alleviating symptoms of GERD.

15. A patient with a history of atrial fibrillation is prescribed warfarin. What is the primary purpose of this medication?

  • Increase blood clotting
  • Prevent blood clots
  • Increase heart rate
  • Reduce heart rate
Correct Answer: B

Warfarin is an anticoagulant used to prevent blood clots, reducing the risk of stroke in patients with atrial fibrillation.
