Nursing RN Study Quiz

1. A patient with a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) presents with increased shortness of breath and wheezing. Which intervention should be prioritized?

  • Administer a sedative
  • Administer a bronchodilator
  • Encourage deep breathing exercises
  • Increase fluid intake
Correct Answer: B

Administering a bronchodilator is crucial for relieving bronchospasm and improving airflow in COPD exacerbations.

2. A patient with diabetes mellitus type 1 is experiencing hypoglycemia. What is the most appropriate initial intervention?

  • Administer insulin
  • Provide a high-protein snack
  • Administer 15g of fast-acting carbohydrate
  • Encourage physical activity
Correct Answer: C

Administering 15g of fast-acting carbohydrate is the recommended initial treatment for hypoglycemia to quickly raise blood glucose levels.

3. A patient with a history of myocardial infarction is prescribed a beta-blocker. What is the primary purpose of this medication?

  • Increase heart rate
  • Increase blood pressure
  • Increase myocardial oxygen demand
  • Decrease myocardial oxygen demand
Correct Answer: D

Beta-blockers decrease myocardial oxygen demand by reducing heart rate and contractility, which is beneficial post-myocardial infarction.
